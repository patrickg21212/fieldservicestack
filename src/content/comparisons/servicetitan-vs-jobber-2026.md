If you've been shopping for <a href="/guides/field-service-management-buyers-guide">field service software</a> this year, you've probably already run into this decision. ServiceTitan or Jobber. These two platforms come up in every conversation, every forum thread, every Reddit post about running a trade business. And for good reason: they're genuinely different tools built for genuinely different businesses.

I've spent time in both platforms. Here's what I actually think.

## What's the Difference Between ServiceTitan and Jobber?

**ServiceTitan targets mid-to-large contractors managing multiple technicians or locations, while Jobber is built for small to mid-size home service businesses that want simplicity without a long implementation process. Jobber starts at $29/month with no contract required. ServiceTitan uses quote-based pricing and requires an annual minimum commitment.**

The short version: Jobber is faster to start, cheaper upfront, and easier to use. ServiceTitan is more powerful, more complex, and built for scale. Neither is universally better. The right answer depends entirely on the size and complexity of your operation.

## Scheduling and Dispatch

**Jobber gives you a clean calendar view with drag-and-drop job management that works well for smaller crews. ServiceTitan's dispatch board is more sophisticated, with multi-day scheduling, route optimization, and detailed crew availability tracking built in. If you're managing 10-plus technicians across zones, ServiceTitan's approach handles that complexity better.**

For a solo operator or a team of three, Jobber's scheduling is refreshingly straightforward. You see what's on, drag jobs around, done. No onboarding specialist required.

ServiceTitan's dispatch tools are serious. Route optimization, crew availability at a glance, recurring job management at scale. I've watched dispatchers in larger shops run 20-plus technicians efficiently from that board. But it takes time to learn, and the workflows are stricter. You can't just move a job without understanding how it affects the downstream sequence.

Small team? Jobber wins on usability. Bigger operation with complex routing needs? ServiceTitan earns its complexity.

## Pricing and Pricebook Control

**ServiceTitan gives you complete control over your pricebook inside the platform. You can update pricing, customize line items, and manage it all in one place. Jobber's pricebook exists, but the ability to customize and update pricing within the software is limited. For businesses managing complex pricing strategies, this gap is real and worth taking seriously.**

This one matters more than it looks. Your pricebook is your margin. If you can't update it easily, you end up with stale pricing, techs quoting off memory, and jobs that look profitable on paper but bleed money in reality.

ServiceTitan's pricebook functionality is deep. You can build it out, keep it current, and trust that what your tech is quoting in the field matches what you approved. Jobber will build a pricebook for you, but the control stays limited once it's set up.

For businesses doing a lot of quoting with tiered pricing or material markups, this is a real differentiator.

## Reporting and Analytics

**ServiceTitan delivers deep reporting on profit by job, cost tracking, and superior QuickBooks integrations for both Online and Desktop. Jobber includes 20-plus built-in reports with solid dashboards for smaller operations, but report customization and the ability to drill into data sources is limited. If you need to verify where your numbers come from, Jobber runs out of depth fast.**

If you're running a business that needs to know the actual profitability of each job, <a href="/reviews/servicetitan-review-2026">ServiceTitan's reporting is in a different league</a>. You can pull job cost data, look at technician performance, compare actual versus estimated labor, and build custom views.

Jobber's built-in reports are solid for what they are. For an owner running a lean operation who needs revenue totals and job status at a glance, they work fine. But when you try to drill down, or when you're questioning whether a number is accurate, you hit a ceiling quickly.

## Inventory Management

**ServiceTitan includes native inventory management built directly into the platform. Jobber does not. If your operation tracks parts, materials, or truck stock, Jobber will require separate inventory software alongside it, which creates manual reconciliation work and increases the chance of errors.**

This gap surprises people who try Jobber in a parts-heavy trade. HVAC shops, plumbing companies with truck stock, electrical contractors managing wire and fixtures: these businesses need inventory tied into job costing. ServiceTitan handles it natively. Jobber doesn't.

If you're in window cleaning or lawn care, this probably doesn't matter much. If you're pulling parts off trucks and reconciling monthly, it matters a lot.

## Mobile App and Technician Experience

**Both platforms have capable mobile apps for field use. ServiceTitan's app includes multiple estimate tiers (Good, Better, Best), GPS tracking, equipment history, and job history. Jobber's app covers core needs but offers more limited equipment history, job history, and payroll approval features.**

Techs generally don't love learning new software. Both apps are functional enough that adoption isn't a disaster, but they're not identical.

ServiceTitan's Good-Better-Best estimate presentation is worth calling out specifically. It works well for selling service agreements or upgrades at the door. Showing a customer three tiered options converts better than a single flat quote. Jobber doesn't have that built in.

The GPS tracking in ServiceTitan also gives dispatch a live view of where every crew is. Jobber is more limited there.

## Setup Time and Onboarding

**<a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">Jobber is significantly faster to get running</a>. Self-serve sign-up, straightforward onboarding, and most businesses are operational within a few days. ServiceTitan requires dedicated onboarding support and implementation that typically takes weeks to months. For a small business owner who needs software working this week, that difference is real.**

I've seen businesses try to implement ServiceTitan without fully accounting for the ramp time. It's not that the software is bad. It's that it's built for a business that has someone who can own the setup process. You need to build your pricebook, configure dispatch workflows, train your team, and do it in a structured sequence.

Jobber's design philosophy is the opposite. Get in, set up basics, start dispatching. Refine as you go. There's no onboarding specialist required because you genuinely don't need one for most use cases.

## Pricing: What You're Actually Paying

**Jobber starts at $29/month with no long-term contract required. ServiceTitan doesn't publish pricing publicly. It's quote-based and scales with technician count, typically requiring engagement with their enterprise sales team. The barrier to entry for Jobber is low enough that a solo operator can start without a major budget commitment.**

| | ServiceTitan | Jobber |
|---|---|---|
| Pricing model | Quote-based, per-technician | $29-$529/month list price |
| Contract | Annual minimum required | No contract required |
| Setup complexity | High, dedicated onboarding | Low, self-serve |
| Inventory management | Built-in native | Not included |
| Pricebook control | Full customization | Limited |

The pricing gap between these two platforms is significant at the small business end. $29/month to get started is accessible. ServiceTitan's model, with an annual minimum and quote-based per-technician pricing, represents a meaningful investment that only makes sense when you're getting proportional value from the advanced features.

For businesses between roughly 5 and 15 technicians, this is where the decision gets hard. Jobber starts feeling limited. ServiceTitan starts feeling necessary. That's the inflection zone where most people struggle with the choice.

## Which Industries Does Each Serve Best?

**ServiceTitan was built around HVAC and adjacent trades and remains strongest there. For cleaning businesses, Jobber is the more natural fit. Both platforms serve plumbing and electrical, but ServiceTitan's architecture handles complex, multi-crew field service operations better. Cleaning operations benefit from Jobber's simpler recurring schedule management and lower administrative overhead.**

<a href="/guides/best-hvac-software-2026">HVAC shops</a> should look seriously at ServiceTitan. It was built for that world. The pricebook depth, the dispatch complexity, the equipment history: all of it reflects years of building specifically for HVAC operators.

Cleaning businesses are a different story. I've talked to enough cleaning operators to know that neither platform is perfect for them. Both miss things: proof of service per visit, subscription billing for recurring residential contracts, booking drop-off recovery. But Jobber's simpler workflows and lower cost fit the cleaning business model better.

Plumbing, electrical, pest control, roofing, landscaping: both platforms work. The deciding factor is your team size and how complex your dispatching actually is.

## User Adoption and Market Presence

Jobber has more than 40,000 users. The volume of app-store reviews backing its easy-to-use reputation reflects real adoption, not just marketing. Both platforms receive strong independent ratings, landing around 4.5 out of 5 stars across review aggregators.

ServiceTitan earns high marks from the enterprise end. Businesses that have been on it for years tend to stay on it. The investment in setup creates switching costs, sure, but it also reflects how embedded the platform becomes in daily operations.

Neither platform is going anywhere. These are mature businesses with real market traction.

## Which One Should You Choose?

Choose ServiceTitan if:
- You manage multiple technicians or locations
- Your pricing strategy requires deep, ongoing customization
- Job costing and profitability analytics are non-negotiable
- You have significant inventory management needs
- You're willing to invest weeks in implementation and training

Choose Jobber if:
- You want to be operational within days, not months
- Transparent, accessible pricing matters to your current budget
- You're a smaller team that values simplicity over feature depth
- You want built-in AI automation that works without extensive configuration
- You prefer month-to-month flexibility over an annual commitment

There's no shame in picking Jobber if you're not ready for ServiceTitan's complexity. And there's no sense staying on Jobber once your operation has grown past what it can handle. The right platform is the one that fits where your business is right now, not where you hope it'll be in three years.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I switch from Jobber to ServiceTitan later if my business grows?

Yes, but plan for it to take time. ServiceTitan migration from another platform typically requires weeks to months of implementation, data migration, and team training. Most businesses that make the switch do so when they hit 8 to 12 technicians and start feeling the reporting and dispatch limitations of Jobber. Budget for that transition before you're in a growth crunch, not during one.

### Does Jobber integrate with QuickBooks?

Jobber integrates with QuickBooks Online. ServiceTitan integrates with both QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop. That distinction matters if your accountant uses Desktop. ServiceTitan's QuickBooks integration is generally rated more robust for users who need to push detailed job costing data into their accounting system. If you're running QuickBooks Desktop alongside field service software, ServiceTitan handles that connection better.

### Is ServiceTitan worth the cost for a 3-person crew?

Probably not. ServiceTitan's quote-based pricing and implementation overhead are designed for businesses at a larger scale. For a 3-person crew, that investment rarely pays off. Jobber at $29/month covers the scheduling, invoicing, and customer communication needs of a small crew without the complexity or cost of enterprise software. Revisit ServiceTitan when you're running 10 or more technicians and the gaps in Jobber start costing you real money.

### Does Jobber have route optimization?

Jobber's routing capabilities are limited compared to ServiceTitan. Jobber offers basic map routing, but ServiceTitan's route optimization and dispatch board are built for managing multiple technicians across zones simultaneously. If route efficiency is central to your profitability, especially with 6 or more technicians running daily routes, ServiceTitan's dispatch tools handle that workload significantly better.
