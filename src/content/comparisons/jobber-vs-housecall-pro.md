## Pricing

Jobber starts at $49/month (Core, 1 user), with Connect at $129/month (up to 5 users) and Grow at $249/month (up to 15 users). Additional users cost $29/month.

Housecall Pro starts at $65/month (Basic, 1 user), with Essentials at $169/month (up to 5 users) and MAX requiring a custom quote for larger teams. Additional users on Essentials cost $40/month.

Jobber is cheaper at every comparable tier. For a 5-person team, the annual difference is roughly $480 to $600 depending on plan selection. For a 10-person team, the gap widens further because Housecall Pro's per-user add-on costs are $11/month higher than Jobber's.

## Scheduling and Dispatch

Both platforms offer drag-and-drop scheduling with calendar views and technician assignment. Jobber's interface is cleaner and faster to navigate. Creating a job, assigning a tech, and setting the time takes fewer clicks. For an owner doing dispatch between jobs, that efficiency matters.

Housecall Pro's scheduling includes a GPS-based dispatch feature that suggests the nearest available technician, which Jobber lacks in its native toolset. When a call comes in and you need to assign it to someone who can get there fast, Housecall Pro's dispatch shows you who is closest and when they will be free. Jobber requires you to check the map and make that decision manually.

For pure scheduling ease with a small team where you know everyone's schedule, Jobber wins. For dispatch optimization in a multi-tech operation where you are assigning 15+ jobs per day across a metro area, Housecall Pro has the edge.

Both platforms support recurring job scheduling for maintenance agreements and regular service visits. Jobber's batch invoicing for recurring jobs is more streamlined. Housecall Pro handles the scheduling well but the invoicing workflow for recurring services has more steps.

## Invoicing and Payments

Jobber's invoicing workflow is tighter. Quote to job to invoice happens in a natural sequence with minimal clicks. A tech creates a quote on-site, the customer approves it through the Client Hub, the quote converts to a job, and the invoice generates automatically on completion. Batch invoicing handles recurring services cleanly. Payment processing runs through Jobber Payments with competitive rates.

Housecall Pro offers similar invoicing basics but adds two features that differentiate it for higher-ticket businesses:

**Instapay** lets you receive funds faster for a fee instead of waiting for standard processing times. During peak season when you have $30,000 in outstanding invoices and payroll is due Friday, getting funds in hours instead of days keeps cash flow healthy.

**Wisetack financing** allows customers to pay in installments. When a plumber quotes $4,200 for a sewer line repair and the homeowner hesitates, offering 12-month financing at the door converts that "let me think about it" into a signed approval. The financing integration is built into the quoting workflow, not a separate tool.

If your average ticket is under $300, these features matter less. If your average ticket regularly exceeds $500 and financing could improve your close rate, Housecall Pro's payment capabilities are a genuine advantage.

## Mobile App

Both apps are functional in the field. Jobber's app is lighter and loads faster on older devices. It does the core work, scheduling, job details, quotes, invoices, and photos, without excess weight. On a 3-year-old phone or in an area with weak signal, Jobber's app performs noticeably better.

Housecall Pro's app includes on-my-way texts to customers, in-app chat with the office, and real-time GPS tracking. The feature gap on mobile favors Housecall Pro, but the performance gap favors Jobber.

If your technicians use current phones on decent networks, Housecall Pro's mobile features are genuinely useful. The automated customer notifications when a tech marks "en route" reduce "where is my tech?" calls to your office. If your crew uses older devices or works in areas with spotty signal, Jobber's lighter footprint matters more.

For photo documentation, both platforms allow in-app photo capture and attachment to jobs. Jobber handles this slightly faster. Housecall Pro's photo integration is functional but occasionally lags on batch uploads.

## Customer Communication

Housecall Pro has a meaningful lead here. Automated appointment reminders, on-my-way notifications, and post-service review requests are more mature and configurable in Housecall Pro. The customer notification flow, from booking confirmation through service completion and follow-up, runs with less manual setup.

Jobber added automated review request functionality in recent updates, closing a gap that previously pushed customer-focused businesses toward Housecall Pro. But Housecall Pro's broader communication suite, including the on-my-way texts and real-time ETA updates, still offers more automation out of the box.

For businesses where customer experience is a competitive differentiator, this matters. A plumbing company that sends automated arrival updates, follows up with a review request 24 hours after service, and sends a thank-you email with a referral offer stands out from competitors who communicate only when the customer calls.

## Customer Support

Jobber provides phone, email, and chat support across all plans. Response times are consistently good and their knowledge base is well-organized. When you have a question at 3 PM on a Wednesday, you can call and talk to someone.

Housecall Pro limits phone support to higher tiers. Basic plan users rely on chat and email, where response times can stretch during peak hours. The support quality is fine when you reach someone, but access on the lower tier is a real limitation.

If you are on the entry-level plan and value being able to pick up the phone when something goes wrong, Jobber's support accessibility across all tiers is a clear advantage.

## Marketing Features

Housecall Pro includes built-in postcard marketing, automated review requests, and a basic website builder. The postcard module lets you send targeted direct mail campaigns for seasonal services, reactivation of lapsed customers, or neighborhood saturation after completing a visible job. The website builder is basic but functional for businesses that do not have a web presence yet.

Jobber added review request automation in recent updates but does not match the breadth of Housecall Pro's marketing toolkit. Jobber does not include postcard marketing, direct mail, or a website builder.

If generating reviews and running direct mail campaigns matters to your business, Housecall Pro ships those features natively. Jobber requires third-party tools for the same outcomes, which adds monthly costs and integration complexity.

For businesses that already have a website and use Google Ads for marketing, this category matters less. Housecall Pro's marketing tools are most valuable for businesses that currently have limited marketing infrastructure and want an all-in-one platform.

## Integrations

Both platforms integrate with QuickBooks Online, though Jobber's integration is available on all paid plans while Housecall Pro gates it behind the Essentials tier ($169/month). This distinction matters if you are on a tight budget and QuickBooks sync is non-negotiable.

Jobber integrates with Stripe, Mailchimp, Zapier, and a growing list of tools. Housecall Pro integrates with QuickBooks, Wisetack, Google Local Services, and various marketing platforms.

Neither platform has the integration depth of <a href="/reviews/servicetitan-review-2026">ServiceTitan</a>, but for most small businesses running QuickBooks and a basic marketing stack, both cover the essentials.

## Verdict

**Choose <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">Jobber</a>** if you prioritize clean scheduling, faster invoicing workflows, lower cost, reliable mobile performance on any device, and phone support from day one. It is the better tool for businesses that want to handle the operational basics without clutter. If your average ticket is under $300 and your primary concern is getting jobs scheduled, completed, and invoiced efficiently, Jobber delivers more value per dollar.

**Choose <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro</a>** if you need dispatch optimization, customer financing, built-in marketing tools, and richer mobile features for your technicians. The higher price buys capabilities that Jobber handles through workarounds or third-party integrations. If your average ticket exceeds $500 and customer financing could improve close rates, or if marketing infrastructure is a current gap, Housecall Pro delivers more out of the box.

For a crew under 5 people doing residential work, Jobber is the stronger value. For a growing operation that wants marketing and financing baked in, Housecall Pro delivers more out of the box.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Jobber or Housecall Pro better for HVAC companies?

<a href="/guides/best-hvac-software-2026">Housecall Pro is generally the better fit for HVAC companies</a>. The customer financing integration (Wisetack) helps close expensive repair and replacement quotes. The GPS dispatch helps manage multi-tech operations across large service areas. The marketing tools fill shoulder-season schedules. Jobber works well for small HVAC shops that prioritize simplicity and cost, but HVAC companies with higher average tickets and seasonal marketing needs get more value from Housecall Pro's feature set.

### Can I switch from Housecall Pro to Jobber or vice versa?

Yes, but expect a transition period. Customer data can be exported and imported between platforms, though the process requires manual mapping in some cases. The bigger friction is retraining your team on the new interface and rebuilding workflow automations. Plan for 1 to 2 weeks of reduced efficiency during the switch. Most businesses that switch do so because of pricing (moving to Jobber to save money) or features (moving to Housecall Pro for financing and marketing).

### Which platform has better online reviews?

Both platforms maintain strong ratings across review sites, typically 4.5 out of 5 stars. Jobber has more total reviews, reflecting its larger user base among small businesses. Housecall Pro reviews tend to highlight the marketing and customer-facing features as strengths. Common complaints about Jobber focus on reporting limitations. Common complaints about Housecall Pro focus on support accessibility on lower tiers and the higher per-user costs.

### Do either Jobber or Housecall Pro offer a free plan?

Neither offers a permanent free plan. Both provide free trial periods with full feature access. Jobber's 14-day trial gives access to Grow-tier features and does not require a credit card. Housecall Pro also offers a trial period. Use both trials in the same week with the same real job data to make a direct comparison under identical conditions.

### Which is better for a solo operator just starting out?

Jobber. The Core plan at $49/month is cheaper than Housecall Pro's Basic at $65/month, and phone support is included from day one. For a solo operator, the advanced features that Housecall Pro offers (GPS dispatch, financing, marketing) matter less when you are the only technician and your primary need is scheduling, quoting, and getting paid. Start with Jobber, build your customer base, and evaluate Housecall Pro when your team and average ticket size grow enough to benefit from the additional features.
