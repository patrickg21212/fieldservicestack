<p><strong>The best invoice software for construction in 2026 is Jobber ($49-$249/month) for small crews needing fast field invoicing, and BuilderTrend ($399+/month) for residential GCs managing full project cycles with milestone billing. ServiceTitan covers large commercial operations. All three sync with QuickBooks and support mobile invoicing from the job site without requiring office staff to operate them.</strong></p>

<p>Paper invoices and emailed PDFs cost contractors real money. Slow payments, disputed charges, and the "I never got that invoice" conversation are fixable problems. What's surprising is how many construction businesses still operate this way in 2026, not because they don't want better tools, but because they picked software that didn't match how they actually work and gave up on it.</p>

<p>This guide covers six options built for construction specifically, tools that understand milestone billing, change orders, job costing, and the reality that most invoicing happens from a phone at the job site, not from a desk back at the office.</p>

<h2>What to Look For in Construction Invoice Software</h2>

<p><strong>Construction invoicing needs features that most generic billing apps skip: progress billing by milestone, change order documentation, job cost tracking tied to your estimates, lien waiver management, and retention holdbacks. The software also needs a functional mobile app for on-site use. If it lacks these, it's an accounting tool that happens to have an invoice button, not a construction tool.</strong></p>

<h3>Progress Billing</h3>

<p>Residential GCs and commercial contractors bill by milestone, not by completed project. Framing done: invoice. Drywall in: invoice. Final walkthrough: invoice. Software that handles this natively saves hours of manual tracking every month. For larger commercial work, AIA-style billing (G702/G703 format) is sometimes required by the owner or the GC above you. Not every tool handles this, so know what your contracts require before you buy anything.</p>

<h3>Change Order Management</h3>

<p>Scope creep kills profit. When a client adds a bathroom, moves a load-bearing wall, or upgrades to tile, you need to document it, price it, get written approval, and attach it to the final invoice. Software that manages this in one place prevents arguments at closeout. If you're currently handling change orders with email threads and Word docs, that's leverage you're giving away for free on every job.</p>

<h3>Job Costing</h3>

<p>You estimated $5,200 in labor for the kitchen remodel. Did you actually hit that number? Job costing tracks actual costs against estimates so you know which job types make money and which ones don't. This matters more than most contractors realize until they look back at a busy year and wonder where the profit went. Some software handles this natively. Others require a QuickBooks workaround that your bookkeeper has to manage manually.</p>

<h3>Mobile Access and Field Usability</h3>

<p>Your crew is at the job site, not at a desk. If the invoice app doesn't work reliably on iOS and Android with spotty cell service, it won't get used. The best options let techs generate invoices, collect digital signatures, and take card payments right at the site. Offline capability matters too. Apps that require a connection to function fail exactly when you need them most.</p>

<h3>QuickBooks and Accounting Integration</h3>

<p>Most construction businesses run QuickBooks for taxes. Whatever invoice software you pick needs a clean, bidirectional sync so you're not manually re-entering data. A broken or clunky integration is a silent time sink that adds hours to monthly bookkeeping and creates reconciliation headaches at tax time. Ask specifically whether the sync is one-way or two-way before you commit.</p>

<h2>The 6 Best Invoice Software Options for Construction in 2026</h2>

<p><strong>The top construction invoice software picks for 2026 are Jobber, BuilderTrend, ServiceTitan, QuickBooks Online, Housecall Pro, and Invoice Ninja. Jobber leads for small trade contractors. BuilderTrend is built for residential GCs with project-based billing cycles. ServiceTitan handles enterprise-scale commercial operations. The others fill specific gaps in price or workflow that the first three don't cover.</strong></p>

<h3>1. Jobber: Best for Small to Mid-Size Crews</h3>

<p>Jobber is the most practical option for contractors running 1 to 15 people. It's built for the field, not the finance department. Quotes convert to jobs, jobs generate invoices, invoices get paid online. That loop runs fast and it runs without IT support or a dedicated admin.</p>

<p>The mobile app is genuinely good. Techs can invoice on-site, collect card payments, and send receipts without touching a laptop. <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">Jobber's full feature set</a> also includes scheduling, dispatch, a client hub, and automated follow-up reminders for unpaid invoices. That last feature alone is worth the price if you spend time every week manually chasing slow-paying clients.</p>

<p>For a 3-truck HVAC or plumbing crew doing service calls and smaller renovation work, Jobber handles everything cleanly. Where it falls short: it's not designed for complex project-based billing. Multi-phase residential construction with draws, lien releases, and subcontractor coordination gets messy in Jobber because the tool wasn't built for that workflow. If that describes your business, look at BuilderTrend instead.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Pricing: Core $49/month (1 user), Connect $149/month (up to 5 users), Grow $249/month (unlimited users)</li>
  <li>Mobile app: Excellent. iOS and Android, offline capable</li>
  <li>QuickBooks sync: Yes, bidirectional</li>
  <li>Best for: Trade contractors, service work, small GCs with straightforward billing</li>
</ul>

<h3>2. BuilderTrend: Best for Residential Construction GCs</h3>

<p>BuilderTrend is built specifically for residential construction. It covers the full project lifecycle: estimates, contracts, schedules, subcontractor coordination, change orders, draws, and final invoicing. If you're a GC running custom homes or large renovations, this is the most complete option available in 2026.</p>

<p>The invoicing module supports progress billing, draws tied to project milestones, and an owner-facing portal where clients can view updates and approve change orders electronically. That client portal alone cuts down the volume of "what's the status" calls on longer projects in a way that's hard to put a dollar figure on but is very real.</p>

<p>The price is real too. Essential starts at $399/month, Advanced is $699/month, and Complete goes up from there. If you're building custom homes averaging $400,000 each, that monthly cost makes sense as a business expense. For remodelers doing smaller jobs with simpler billing, the math gets harder to justify. BuilderTrend is a serious tool built for serious volume.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Pricing: Essential $399/month, Advanced $699/month, Complete $999/month</li>
  <li>Mobile app: Good, full feature access on iOS and Android</li>
  <li>QuickBooks sync: Yes, including job costing integration</li>
  <li>Best for: Custom home builders, residential GCs, large remodelers</li>
</ul>

<h3>3. ServiceTitan: Best for Large Commercial and Multi-Location Operations</h3>

<p>ServiceTitan is the enterprise option. Pricing isn't published (expect $250-$600+ per tech per month based on what contractors consistently report), and onboarding takes weeks. But if you're running 20-plus techs across multiple locations or managing commercial contracts with complex billing requirements, nothing else in this category comes close for raw capability.</p>

<p>The invoicing, job costing, and reporting capabilities are deep. <a href="/reviews/servicetitan-review-2026">ServiceTitan's reporting suite</a> gives you margin visibility by technician, by job type, and by location. That kind of data matters when you're managing scale and need to know which service lines are actually profitable versus which ones you're running out of habit. The trade-off is complexity, onboarding cost, and a monthly bill that will hurt in the early stages of any business.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Pricing: Custom (typically $300-$600+/user/month based on contractor-reported data)</li>
  <li>Mobile app: Excellent, purpose-built for field techs</li>
  <li>QuickBooks sync: Yes, plus a native accounting module</li>
  <li>Best for: Large commercial contractors, multi-location operations, high-volume trade businesses</li>
</ul>

<h3>4. QuickBooks Online: Best When the Accountant Runs the Books</h3>

<p>QuickBooks Online is not field service software. It's accounting software with decent invoicing built in. A lot of small construction outfits use it because their bookkeeper or CPA already lives in QuickBooks and the integration question disappears entirely.</p>

<p>The invoicing side works. You can create estimates, convert them to invoices, track payments, and attach receipts. What you don't get: dispatch, scheduling, mobile job management, or any workflow built for a crew in the field. It's a back-office tool that contractors sometimes try to use as a front-office tool, and that gap shows up fast once the jobs start piling up.</p>

<p>For a solo GC or a very small operation where the owner handles everything and hands receipts to an accountant monthly, QuickBooks Online at $30-$90/month is hard to argue against. If you have any field complexity, pair it with a field service app or switch to something built for construction workflows.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Pricing: Simple Start $30/month, Essentials $60/month, Plus $90/month, Advanced $200/month</li>
  <li>Mobile app: Adequate for basic invoicing, weak on field-specific features</li>
  <li>QuickBooks sync: Native (it is QuickBooks)</li>
  <li>Best for: Solo contractors, bookkeeper-managed businesses, simple billing needs</li>
</ul>

<h3>5. Housecall Pro: Best for Trade Service Contractors</h3>

<p>Housecall Pro is a strong pick for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and pest control contractors whose construction work leans toward service calls and smaller jobs rather than multi-month projects. If that describes your operation, it fits the daily workflow better than most options on this list.</p>

<p>The invoicing workflow is fast. Techs invoice on-site, customers pay by card or ACH, and everything syncs to QuickBooks automatically. There's also a built-in review request system, which helps with Google review volume if you're building local visibility. <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro's full capabilities</a> go deeper on scheduling and dispatch, but for a busy service operation the invoicing speed alone justifies the monthly cost.</p>

<p>The gap is clear: it's not designed for project-based construction. Milestone billing, draws, and lien management are not part of the Housecall Pro feature set. For service-oriented work, that's irrelevant. For a GC running multi-phase projects, it's a dealbreaker.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Pricing: Basic $69/month (1 user), Essentials $169/month (up to 5 users), MAX pricing on request</li>
  <li>Mobile app: One of the best in field service</li>
  <li>QuickBooks sync: Yes</li>
  <li>Best for: HVAC, plumbing, electrical, pest control, cleaning contractors</li>
</ul>

<h3>6. Invoice Ninja: Best Free Option for Very Small Operations</h3>

<p>Invoice Ninja is the zero-cost starting point. The free plan handles up to 5 clients with unlimited invoices. Pro runs $12/month for unlimited clients. It's not construction-specific and the feature set is thin, but it covers the basics: create invoice, send invoice, collect payment online.</p>

<p>For a solo subcontractor or someone just starting out who needs to stop sending invoices through email text, Invoice Ninja gets the job done without spending anything. It won't scale far, but it's a legitimate first step. For a broader look at low-cost options in this range, the <a href="/guides/cheapest-field-service-software">cheapest field service software guide</a> covers this tier in detail and includes a few other budget-friendly picks worth comparing.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Pricing: Free (up to 5 clients), Pro $12/month, Enterprise $16/month</li>
  <li>Mobile app: Basic, functional for simple invoicing</li>
  <li>QuickBooks sync: Limited, some manual export required</li>
  <li>Best for: Solo operators, early-stage contractors, very low volume</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to Choose the Right Invoice Software for Your Construction Business</h2>

<p><strong>Match the software to your billing model, not just your crew size. Service contractors who bill per visit need fast mobile invoicing and on-site payment collection. GCs who bill by milestone need progress billing and change order documentation. Contractors whose accountant manages everything often do fine starting with QuickBooks Online and adding a field service layer when volume demands it.</strong></p>

<h3>Running a Service Business (HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical, Pest Control)</h3>

<p>Jobber or Housecall Pro. Both are built for high-volume service calls where you invoice per visit and collect payment on-site. The differences in pricing, dispatch features, and day-to-day workflow are worth understanding before you commit. The <a href="/comparisons/jobber-vs-housecall-pro">Jobber vs Housecall Pro breakdown</a> goes deep on where each one wins and where it falls short. Pick based on which interface your techs will actually use without complaining about it, because adoption is the real make-or-break factor with any of these tools.</p>

<h3>Running Residential General Contracting</h3>

<p>BuilderTrend if you're doing high-value projects (average $150K or more) with complex billing cycles involving draws and milestone approvals. Jobber if you're doing remodels and smaller jobs where the billing is relatively straightforward. The $350/month price gap between the two is significant. Be honest about whether you actually need everything BuilderTrend offers before you commit to it for 12 months.</p>

<h3>Starting Out or Going Solo</h3>

<p>QuickBooks Online Simple Start at $30/month or Invoice Ninja's free plan. Get the basics working first. When you're turning away work because admin overhead is eating your time, that's when you upgrade. Don't over-invest in software infrastructure before you have the volume to justify the cost. Every dollar of software spending is a dollar that doesn't go toward materials or payroll.</p>

<h3>Running a Large Operation (20-Plus Techs)</h3>

<p>ServiceTitan. The cost is real, but so is the reporting depth and the billing complexity it handles. Running 30 techs through Jobber works up to a point, then falls apart under the volume and starts costing you visibility into your own business. The transition to ServiceTitan is painful, but doing it late costs more in lost efficiency than doing it early costs in setup time.</p>

<h2>Our Recommendation</h2>

<p><strong>For most construction contractors in 2026, Jobber is the right starting point. It handles mobile invoicing, online payments, job tracking, and QuickBooks sync at a price that works for crews up to 15 people. Move to BuilderTrend when you're managing multi-phase projects with milestone billing and subcontractor coordination. Skip ServiceTitan until you're at genuine scale with someone dedicated to running it.</strong></p>

<p>The biggest mistake contractors make with software is buying for where they want to be instead of where they are. Jobber at $149/month handles a 5-person crew without friction. BuilderTrend at $399/month is wasted if you're doing simple remodel work without real project management complexity. Start right, then upgrade when the workflow actually demands it.</p>

<p>For trade contractors doing service-oriented construction work, HVAC, plumbing, and electrical in particular, Housecall Pro is a legitimate alternative to Jobber at a comparable price point. The mobile invoicing is excellent and the client-facing experience is clean. Either way, the best invoice software is the one your crew actually uses on every job, not the one with the longest feature list sitting unused on a phone screen nobody touches.</p>
<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: Can I invoice directly from the job site on my phone?</h3>
<p>Yes—Jobber, BuilderTrend, and ServiceTitan all feature offline mobile invoicing that syncs automatically when you're back online. This eliminates the "I never got that invoice" excuse and lets crews send invoices the same day work is completed, speeding up payment cycles significantly.</p>
<h3>Q: What's the actual cost difference between Jobber and BuilderTrend?</h3>
<p>Jobber runs $49–$249/month depending on crew size, while BuilderTrend starts at $399+/month for residential GCs managing multiple ongoing projects. The pricing jump reflects BuilderTrend's built-in job costing, full project management, and native milestone billing—features Jobber requires add-ons for.</p>
<h3>Q: How does milestone billing actually prevent payment disputes?</h3>
<p>Milestone billing lets you invoice at specific completion points (framing done, drywall in, final walkthrough) instead of one lump sum, so both you and the client see clear expectations tied to completed work. Construction invoice software automatically tracks which phases are billable and generates documentation to back up each invoice.</p>
<h3>Q: Does construction invoice software actually integrate with QuickBooks, or is that just marketing?</h3>
<p>All three major options sync directly with QuickBooks—invoices and payments flow automatically without manual re-entry. The integration is typically plug-and-play, though you'll want to confirm your specific QuickBooks version (Desktop vs. Online) is supported during setup to avoid surprises.</p>
