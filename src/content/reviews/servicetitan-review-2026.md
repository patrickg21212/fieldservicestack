## The Feature Set

### Dispatching

ServiceTitan covers nearly everything a mid-to-large service company needs. Dispatching is the core strength. The dispatch board gives real-time technician locations, job status, and schedule density at a glance. Smart dispatch recommendations factor in technician skills, location proximity, and job priority. For companies running 20+ techs across a metro area, this is where ServiceTitan justifies its cost.

The dispatch board supports capacity planning, zone-based routing, and real-time schedule adjustments. When an emergency call comes in, the system identifies the nearest qualified technician who can absorb the job without blowing out the rest of their day. For a dispatcher managing 30+ daily jobs, this intelligence eliminates the guesswork and phone calls that manual dispatch requires.

Automated customer communications tied to dispatch status keep homeowners informed. When a tech marks "en route," the customer receives an arrival window. This reduces no-shows and "where's my technician?" calls to your office.

### Marketing Attribution

The marketing scorecard ties ad spend directly to booked revenue. You can see which campaigns generate calls, which calls convert to jobs, and which jobs produce actual profit. Most field service platforms punt on marketing attribution entirely. ServiceTitan treats it as a first-class feature.

For a company spending $10,000/month on Google Ads, LSA, and direct mail, knowing that the LSA campaign generates $45,000 in booked revenue while the direct mail generates $3,000 changes your budget allocation immediately. This data is not theoretical. It connects phone calls to customer records to completed jobs to invoiced revenue.

### Pricebook Management

Pricebook management lets you maintain standardized pricing across your entire operation. Technicians see approved prices on their tablets, customers get consistent quotes, and your margins stay where you set them. Price updates push to the field in real time.

For <a href="/guides/best-hvac-software-2026">HVAC</a> and plumbing companies that live by flat rate pricing, this feature eliminates the margin erosion that happens when techs quote from memory or use outdated price lists. One centralized pricebook means your $1,200 compressor replacement is $1,200 every time, from every tech, at every property.

### Memberships and Service Agreements

The membership module handles recurring maintenance contracts with automated billing and renewal tracking. For businesses where maintenance agreements represent recurring revenue, this module manages the full lifecycle: sign-up, billing, renewal reminders, and scheduled service visits.

Renewal tracking is particularly valuable. When 200 maintenance agreements come up for renewal in the same quarter, automated reminders and simplified renewal workflows prevent the revenue leakage that happens when renewals slip through manual processes.

### Additional Features

Phone integration records inbound calls, links them to customer records, and scores CSR performance. This creates accountability for how your office team handles calls and reveals conversion rates at the phone stage.

Payroll processing, equipment tracking, permit management, and a built-in financing option for customers round out the feature list. The financing option works similarly to Housecall Pro's Wisetack integration, allowing techs to offer payment plans at the door.

Inventory management is built in natively, tracking parts, materials, and truck stock tied to job costing. For trades that manage significant inventory, this eliminates the need for a separate system and prevents the reconciliation headaches that come with managing inventory outside your field service platform.

## Where ServiceTitan Falls Short

### Learning Curve and Implementation

The learning curve is steep. Implementation typically takes 8 to 12 weeks with dedicated onboarding support. Your dispatchers, CSRs, and technicians all need training. Expect productivity to drop for the first month as your team adjusts. Small companies that rush the implementation often blame the software when the problem was the rollout.

This is the most common source of ServiceTitan dissatisfaction. The platform is powerful, but power requires investment in learning. Companies that dedicate someone to own the implementation and run structured training see significantly better outcomes than those that hand login credentials to their team and expect them to figure it out.

### Mobile App Weight

The mobile app for technicians (Titan Pro) is feature-rich but heavy. It demands a reasonably current tablet or phone and a reliable data connection. On older devices or in areas with poor connectivity, the experience degrades noticeably. Load times increase, transitions lag, and techs get frustrated.

Compare this to <a href="/reviews/jobber-review-2026">Jobber's mobile app</a>, which runs smoothly on 3-year-old phones with weak signal. For companies equipping techs with current hardware and working in metro areas, this limitation is minor. For rural operations or companies using older devices, it is a real friction point.

### Configuration Trap

Customization can become a trap. ServiceTitan is so configurable that companies sometimes spend months tweaking workflows instead of running them. The platform works best when you commit to their recommended setup and adjust incrementally rather than rebuilding every process from scratch during onboarding.

The temptation to customize everything up front is strong. Resist it. Get operational on ServiceTitan's defaults first. Customize after you understand how the platform handles your daily workflow. Companies that over-customize during implementation create complexity that confuses their team and delays the return on investment.

### Reporting Paradox

Reporting is powerful but requires investment to use well. The data is there, but building the dashboards and reports that actually matter to your business takes time and someone who understands what to measure.

Out of the box, ServiceTitan's reports show everything. The challenge is filtering signal from noise. A 15-tech HVAC company does not need 50 dashboards. They need 5 to 7 reports that track the metrics that drive their specific business. Building those requires time and thought.

## Verdict

ServiceTitan is the strongest platform for established field service companies with 15 or more technicians and the budget to invest in proper implementation. The dispatching, marketing attribution, and pricebook management are genuinely best-in-class. But the price, learning curve, and implementation timeline make it a poor fit for small crews or companies that need to be productive on day one. If you are running a lean operation under 10 techs, look at <a href="/comparisons/servicetitan-vs-jobber-2026">Jobber</a> or <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro-review-2026">Housecall Pro</a> first. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is ServiceTitan worth the cost?

ServiceTitan is worth the cost for companies with 15+ technicians doing $2M+ in annual revenue where the dispatch intelligence, marketing attribution, and pricebook management generate measurable ROI. For a 20-tech HVAC company, reducing average drive time by 15 minutes per tech per day and improving close rates by 10% through better pricing presentation can generate $100,000+ in annual value against a $50,000-$70,000 annual software cost. For smaller operations, the math rarely works.

### How long does ServiceTitan take to set up?

Plan for 8 to 12 weeks from contract signing to full operational use. This includes pricebook build-out, dispatch workflow configuration, team training, data migration, and integration setup. A dedicated project manager from ServiceTitan guides the process. Expect productivity to dip in the first month after go-live as your team adjusts. Companies that rush implementation consistently report lower satisfaction with the platform.

### Can a small company use ServiceTitan?

Technically yes. ServiceTitan does not have a minimum technician requirement. But the per-technician pricing ($245-$398/month), annual commitment, and implementation investment make it financially impractical for companies under 10 techs. A 5-tech operation would pay $15,000-$24,000/year for software that delivers most of the same daily outcomes as Jobber at $3,000-$4,000/year. The advanced features that justify ServiceTitan's cost only generate returns at scale.

### Does ServiceTitan work for industries beyond HVAC?

ServiceTitan was originally built for HVAC and remains strongest there. It now supports plumbing, electrical, garage door, roofing, water treatment, and other home service trades. The dispatch, pricebook, and marketing features apply broadly. However, some industry-specific features (like chemical tracking for pest control or route density optimization for lawn care) are better served by specialized platforms like Service Autopilot or PestRoutes.

### What are the main alternatives to ServiceTitan?

For businesses not ready for ServiceTitan's cost and complexity, Housecall Pro ($79-$189/month) offers a lighter platform with financing and marketing tools. FieldPulse ($99/month + per-user fees) provides flat rate pricebooks and job costing at a fraction of the price. Jobber ($49-$249/month) handles core scheduling and invoicing for small teams. For enterprise competitors at ServiceTitan's level, Aspire (now owned by ServiceTitan) targets commercial landscape and building services.
